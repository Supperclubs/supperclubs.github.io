Session #3

What is responsible consumption of information, news, and culture?

Attention, algorithms, and what we owe our minds.

Friday, 20 March 2026 7:00 PM TBS

The Question

We scroll, we skim, we binge. Algorithms feed us what holds our attention, not what nourishes our understanding. Meanwhile, we're drowning in information but starving for meaning. What does it look like to be a responsible consumer of news, media, and culture? Is it even possible in a system designed to exploit our attention? We want to ask what we owe our own minds—and whether the answer changes how we live.

Questions We Explored

  • Is there a moral duty to be well-informed—or is "staying informed" itself a trap?
  • What would it look like to consume media with intention rather than impulse?
  • Are we responsible for the effects of algorithms on our thinking, or are we victims of them?
  • What's the difference between entertainment and enrichment—and does it matter?
