What is this?

Picture a long table, warm light, good food. Around it, ten people who came as strangers and are slowly becoming friends.

Someone asks a question that doesn't have an easy answer. The room gets quiet for a moment—then alive. That's Supper Club.

Once a month, at someone's home. A reading beforehand, a conversation over dinner, and the kind of evening you think about for days after.

The kind of questions we ask

For example...

"Should everyone have the right to vote, regardless of knowledge or engagement?"
"Should future generations have political representation today?"
"What idea feels hardest for you to defend out loud?"
"What would you lose if society became more just?"
